“I’ve found all of my team to be receptive when I’ve taken the time to explain the principles that guide my decisions.”

It doesn’t matter if you are talking UX or coding, if you come in and say “do what I say because I’m an expert” you will get poor responses. If instead you say “do what I am saying because X,Y,Z” you may get people questioning your reasons, but they will engage with you.

this goes hand-in-hand with the problem you refer to in establishing credibility. There are people out there who claim to be UX experts, but who completley kill products because they design for a different set of users than the ones who actually use the software.

look at the gnome project for a long series of flamewars about this topic.
